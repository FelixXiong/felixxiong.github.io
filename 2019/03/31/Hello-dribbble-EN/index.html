On March 22, 2019, I finally got the Dribbble account that the designer dreamed of.

This is a brand new starting point on my design road.

My Dribbble homepage: PenghaoXiong

Design ideas

When I was in college, I was dream to have a dribbble account, not only for work, but also to prove my strengths in design and the ability to learn.

Later is to show my best design skills.

First shot

Clearly

This is my First shot clearly.

As usual, every new designer needs to make a debut.

The following is a simple sharing of my design ideas, the creation time is 5 days(In actual projects, this time is usually scheduled).

Process

Asking some questions

First of all, the software you need.

Maybe any software can do it: PhotoShop, inllustrator, C4D, even Keynote…

Secondly, how can I do this “first show”/debut?

set a strategy

Some design options were previously considered:

C4D made a rocket, opened the door to a new planet and landed, and found that everyone was dribbling;

Create a shooting animation, then use the camera to print, prove First shot…

Location: T.I.T Creative Park near Guangzhou Kecun, in the number of living museums [1].

After a number of design proposals, it was finally decided to design in the form of a ticket.

Perhaps because of their love for the iOS app “card package” and card-style design [2], and the desire to make a different feeling, the viewer can let themselves explore the feeling.

The components of a ticket: start-end point, inviter, design highlights.

What is the difference

Here is an example, arranged in order.

Ticket split

With reference to the design method of the lottery ticket, the innovation here is decided. I use Morse code.

Because his shape is very much like a dividing line, I also think this is a very innovative “segmentation” design.

Use a long bar symbol and use a dot shape for white to make a dot design.

For whom？

Design for what

Because of the great interest in mobile Internet products, I have become a thing that I create (which will be a product) and establish user goals (although dribbble may have a lot of pure visuals, but this does not affect my The first show design purpose).

So, this became a key point in my first show and an integral part of my animation.

In my previous graduation project, I created a design for caring for the visually impaired in the hospital: the sensory vision device in the hospital [1:1], and the element of global design concept in “Hello, Welcome” [^ 3].

**The answer is to design for everyone. **

Everyone’s “Braille”

Combining tickets with Braille is probably something that no one in dribbble’s pure visual platform has ever done. It’s just a design in early banknotes, or it’s just a matter of some things.

Because of pure vision, visually impaired people may rarely watch dribbble, but the designer is not excluded.

Why can’t I add Braille to dribbble invation?

In order to have a more visual “feel”, a dark gray gradient color is used.

The two words are “Dribbble Ticket” and “Hello dribbble”.
